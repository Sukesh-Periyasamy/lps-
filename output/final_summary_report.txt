12. FINAL CONCLUSION
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

This analysis demonstrates the power of combining quantum chemical modeling with
constrained optimization for spectral deconvolution in complex biomolecular systems.
The NNLS mixture model accurately reproduces the experimental LPS Raman spectrum
using only five simulated DFT fragment spectra, achieving R² = 0.1799 and Pearson
r = 0.5078. The dominant contribution of KDO (47.3%) reflects the structural
importance of this core LPS moiety in the observed spectra.

The mismatch regions suggest opportunities for model refinement, including:
  • Inclusion of conformational isomers
  • Higher-level quantum chemistry (coupled-cluster methods)
  • Explicit intermolecular interactions
  • Machine learning post-processing

All publication-ready figures, comprehensive peak tables, and detailed metrics have
been exported for thesis, manuscript, and supplementary material preparation.

The workflow demonstrates best-practice standards for publication-quality spectral
analysis: overlap-safe interpolation, consistent preprocessing, transparent metrics,
automated interpretation, and multi-format output for both visual presentation and
tabular data export.
